What you'll need

A 3D printer. I did mine with a " target="supplies">Creality Ender 3.
" target="supplies">Black and " target="supplies">White filament
A " target="supplies">Raspberry Pi, any model up to the latest model 3 B+ will do.
A small " target="supplies">MicroSD card, 16 Gb will be more than enough.
A " target="supplies">compatible USB gamepad and a " target="supplies">wireless mini keyboard are strongly recommended, but not a requisite.
A micro USB power supply with a little more juice than the standard Raspberry Pi one, because we'll be driving a screen too. 3 Amps will be enough, " target="supplies">like this one.
The " target="supplies">Waveshare 2,8 inch. screen for Raspberry Pi, the exact model is important here.
" target="supplies">26-lane IDC ribbon cable with connector to connect the screen to the Raspberry Pi.
" target="supplies">A male IDC 26 pin socket like this to make it easier to plug and unplug the display.
Screws.
A panel mount switch button that fits the 15x10 mm. rectangular hole in the Base, " target="supplies">this one fits perfectly.
A micro USB cable extender with panel mount. The Base is prepared for one with a 28mm. distance between the mounting screw holes, " target="supplies">this one fits perfectly.
Two " target="supplies">tiny round 10 x 2mm magnets for that satisfying clicky sound when it closes.
The usual simple soldering stuff, glue and some patience.
