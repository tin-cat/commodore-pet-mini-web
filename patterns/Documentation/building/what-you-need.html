What you'll need

  1. A 3D printer. I did mine with this one.
  2. Black and White filament.
  3. A Raspberry Pi, any model from A to B+ will do.
  4. A small MicroSD card, 16 Gb will be more than enough.
  5. A compatible USB gamepad is strongly recommended, but not a requisite.
  6. A micro USB power supply with a little more juice than the standard Raspberry Pi one, because we'll be driving a screen too, like this one.
  7. This specific screen, because size matters here.
  8. 26 male to female dupont cables to connect the screen to the Raspberry Pi
  9. Screws
  10. A panel mount switch button.
  11. A micro USB panel mount cable extender.
  12. Some tiny round 3 x 1mm magnets for that satisfying clicky sound when it closes.
  13. The usual simple soldering stuff, glue and some patience.
